# Search Intent Taxonomy

The basis for grouping keywords "by intent." Intent determines the article type and funnel position.

## Four Search Intents

| Intent | What the user wants | Matching content type | Funnel |
|---|---|---|---|
| Informational | Understand a concept | Tutorial / definition / guide | TOFU |
| Commercial | Compare solutions, find options | Comparisons, listicles, frameworks | MOFU |
| Transactional / decision | Ready to act | Consulting page, case study, ROI, quote | BOFU |
| Navigational | Find a specific brand/page | Brand page | — |

## Three Grouping Versions You Can Offer

1. Pure-intent version: one group for each of the four intents.
2. Intent × funnel matrix version: split each intent further into TOFU/MOFU/BOFU, useful for filling gaps.
3. Intent × five-pillar version: map onto the decision-first five pillars, plugging directly into topic selection.

## Five Content Pillars (decision-first; B2B priority)

1. **AI decision-making mindset**: making better judgments amid uncertainty (TOFU thought leadership; brand core)
2. **Process as decision**: the decision points of rebuilding workflows with AI (B2B core / authority)
3. **Building AI partners that think**: agents that ask the right question first before answering (builder credibility)
4. **The critical choices of AI adoption**: what to automate, when, and why (B2B BOFU / decision)
5. **Building products with AI**: every step is a decision (practitioner, strong AEO)

## Quality Checks

- Groups are MECE, no overlap.
- The BOFU group is non-empty (funnel goal = B2B discovery).
- Each version flags its pros/cons and the situations it fits.
