# Ceiling-Article Features (Breakdown Table)

Corresponds to the green flowchart node "Ceiling-Article Features." A ceiling article = the strongest content in that keyword's SERP / AI citations. Break down 2–3 of them.

## Per-Article Breakdown

- Title and angle
- Structure (H2/H3 skeleton)
- List of key points covered
- Depth signals (data, case studies, charts, firsthand experience)
- Format (listicle / tutorial / comparison / case study)
- Why it ranks at the top (intent match? authority? depth?)
- Gaps (what's missing, outdated, or unclear) — **specifically hunt for the gap where "an answer is given but the underlying decisions and trade-offs are never discussed."**

## Synthesized Output

- The "entry bar" for this topic: the minimum you must do to be taken seriously.
- Shared gaps: where all the ceiling articles fall short → Zynkr's entry point (the most valuable is the gap where "no one is decision-first").
- AEO observation: who the AI engines currently cite, and why.

## Reminder

The bar exists to be "surpassed," not "copied." Zynkr wins through a decision-first perspective (ask the right question first, surface the trade-offs, give a direction); firsthand experience is only supporting evidence, not a paraphrase of the ceiling article.
